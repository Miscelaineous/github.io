My 3 favourite websites

BBC News is a global and national (UK) news service.

Linked In is a global career and contact repository for professionals.

SkillCrush is a technology educational site, promoting the improvement of coding skills within the female population.

Why are these your favorite sites?

This is my key source for news within the UK and around the world. It's easy to navigate, is smart about its headlines and content, and informative. It is visually easy to read and interpret and with no advertising, is 'noise-free'.

This is my key source of updates regarding work contacts, colleagues (old and new) and friends. It keep me appraised of key professional activities and helps me to reach out to new resources and keep in touch with old ones. It is easy to use and its search facility, enabling me to track down resource and contacts from all over the world, is impressive.

This female-focused website, largely written by women for women, is presented in an easy to understand and non-geeky way which results in coding fundamentals being easier to understand and absorb.

What area of the site is your eye drawn to when looking at the homepage? Stand back further, what area is your eye drawn to now? Is that area the most important area of the site?

I am immediately drawn to the burgundy banner and BBC emblem, which generate a comforting response from me as I recognise and identify with the brand. Regarless of where I am in the world, I find it brings me a sense of attachment to what is going on in the world and at home.

As Linked In comprises my contacts, colleagues and friends, I am immediately drawn to their photos and posts, updating me (and all) of their professional thoughts, activities and events.

I like the simplicity of the SkillCrush site, it's orange colouring and simple, minimalist text and easy navigation.
